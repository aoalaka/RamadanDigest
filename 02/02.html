Some Categories of People Regarding Fasting

Excerpts from Majaalisu-shahri ramadhaan by Shaykh Ibn-l-'uthaymeen

Translated by Abdulquadri Alaka

Ramadan 19, 1434 | May 24, 2019

All praises and adoration are due to Allah. Peace and blessings of Allah be upon the best of mankind, Muhammad, his households, his companions and the generality of the muslims.

A mature, mentally fit, healthily strong, free (from all factors impeding fasting), resident Muslim

The ruling is that fasting is compulsory on him and he has to observe it at its due time. The Quran, the Sunnah and the consensus of scholars supported this ruling. Allah says:

The month of Ramadân in which was revealed the Qur'ân, a guidance for mankind and clear proofs for the guidance and the criterion (between right and wrong). So whoever of you sights (the crescent on the first night of) the month (of Ramadân i.e. is present at his home), he must observe Saum (fasts) that month,…

in Suuratul-Baqarah, verse 185

The prophet sallallahu alayhi wasallam also said “ whenever you see the crescent (indicating the beginning of Ramadan as a month), start fasting.” (Bukhari and Muslim). Likewise, the whole Muslim world agreed that fasting is compulsory on this category of persons.

On the other hand, fasting is not compulsory on a disbeliever. If he chooses to fast, it will not be acceptable from him because he is yet to be certified for Allah’s worship. In other words, accepting Islaam comes first. On this note, if a disbeliever should accept Islam during the month of Ramadan after the start of the month, it is not binding on him to repay the missed days. Allah says:

Say to those who have disbelieved, if they cease (from disbelief) their past will be forgiven.

Suuratul-Anfaal, verse 38

If he should accept Islam during the day (i.e. after fajr) during the month of Ramadan, he is to refrain from eating and drinking till the time of iftaar (time for breaking fast). This because he has become of those on whom fasting is compulsory after accepting Islam. However, it is not compulsory that he should repay the fasting of that day because as at the time the fasting started that day, he was yet to be a believer.

A Muslim child that has not reached maturity age

The ruling is that fasting is not compulsory on him until he matures. The prophet (S.A.W) said, “the pen has been lifted up for three (1) a sleeping person until he wakes up (2) a child until he matures (3) a mentally sick person until he regains his mental healthiness. (At-Tirmidhi 1423 - Al-Huduud; Ahmad vol1. Pg 158).

Nevertheless, it was part of the traditions of the pious predecessors as guardians and parents of children to command them to fast so that they can find fasting easy and customary when they mature. Today, many Muslim parents on the contrary, prevent their children from fasting believing that, it is a way to show mercy to these children. In reality, showing mercy to these children is to train them from childhood with the pristine Islamic education. Whosoever prevents these children from learning this education has indeed become unjust to them. But, if after these children have fasted and the parents noticed that it is becoming uneasy on them, then, they can prevent them from fasting.

A male child becomes mature having experience one of three.

  1. Ejaculation, as a result of wet dream or otherwise. (See Suuratun Nuur, verse 59)
  2. Growing of hairs around body private parts. ‘Atiyyah Al- Quradhiyy said: “On the day of Quraydhah, we were presented to the Prophet (S.A.W) and anyone who was mature among us, whether he has experienced ejaculation or grown pubic hairs, was executed while the immature ones were left”. (At-Tirmidhi, As-sayr, 1584; An-Nasaai, At-Talaq, 3429).
  3. Attainment of 15 years old. Abdullah bin Umar said,” I was presented to the Prophet (S.A.W) as a child of 14 years old on the day of Uhud. He did not grant me permission to follow them to the Battle”. (Al-Bukhari-As-shahaadaat, 2521). Al-Bayhaqi and Ibn-Hibban added, that Ibn Umar further said: “the Prophet (S.A.W) did not see me as being mature. So, when it was the day of Khandaq, I have reached 15 years old, he granted me exam permission.”

In addition to the three mentioned for a male child, a female child reaches maturity whenever she starts experiencing menstruation. Whenever she begins to menstruate, the compulsion falls on her, even if she has not reached 10 years of age. If she attains maturity in the afternoon on a fasting day, she would complete the fast but she does not have to repay that day.

A mentally sick person.

Because is not mentally fit, he is not conscious of his senses. Consequently, fasting is not compulsory on him as stated in the hadith of the prophet (S.A.W) about three for whom the pen has been lifted. If he fasts, it would not be correct. Because he is not conscious of his actions, he is not able to make intentions before actions and actions are built on intentions. If he only gets sick mentally sometimes, not every time, fasting is only compulsory on him, whenever he is mentally sick. If he becomes mentally sick in the afternoon on the day of fasting while he is fasting, his fasting is still valid because he intended fasting and started it in a conscious state. There is no evidence indicating the voidness of his fasting, especially when the period of his mental sickness can be defined. Therefore, it is not compulsory on him to repay that day. This is also applicable to someone who fainted while fasting as a result of sickness or otherwise

If a mentally sick person becomes healthy or regain his mental healthiness in the afternoon on the day of fasting, he is to refrain from fasting till iftaar time but he is not to repay that day. His case is similar to a child getting mature or a disbeliever accepting Islam as mentioned above.

An old person whose agedness has made him lose his sense or faculty.

The ruling is that fasting is not compulsory on him and he is not to feed, in lieu of the fast. Losing his consciousness or distinguishing factor puts him in the position of a child that is yet to mature. However, if he regains his consciousness sometimes, then fasting becomes compulsory, only when is conscious.

A weak or sick person that is not expected to recover.

The ruling is that fasting is not compulsory on him. He is only to feed a poor person in lieu of every single day he misses.

O Allah, make us understand your religion and inspire us to know the secret behind your legislations. Make our affairs in both lives perfect. Forgive us, our parents and all the Muslims, for You are the most merciful of the merciful ones. O Allah, shower Your blessings upon the prophet, his households and his companions in general.
